K-means clustering

Outline

  • Non-probabilistic method of clustering data points

Method

  • The algorithm can be easily understood in two dimensions, and is simply expandable to any number of dimensions.
  • We first define k prototypes, which are mu_k - we can choose these randomly, as the iterative process will ensure their convergence (eventually!)

The algorithm is very straightforward:

  • Choose the initial k prototypes
  • We produce r_nk for each k. E.g. if we have k=2 and 6 data points, our r_nks will look something like:
  • We update mu_k for all k. In the above example, this would mean taking the 2nd and 3rd data points (where the indicator variable was 1 for r1), and our updated mu_k would be the average of these two data points. We calculate this for all k and get our new centroids (mu_k).
  • We then repeat this process until there's no change in the indicator variables. The centre of our clusters are the current mu_k, and the data points in each cluster are given by the indicators.
